Problem Identification

Handwashing dishes is a common task in global households. In observing how people handwash their dishes, we noticed the majority leave their sink faucet running for the duration of the activity. "It's a 3-armed job: one to hold the plate, one to hold a sponge, and another to control the water. Guess which one loses out?"

Several products already exist for hands-free faucet controls: infrared 'automatic faucets' and foot pedal controls are the most common. These products have various drawbacks:

  • expensive: many of those who have the most to gain from this product have limited purchasing power
  • complicated to install: many potential customers lack the experience or property rights to make significant changes to their kitchen
  • erratic and unreliable: we want to empower our users, not get in their way
